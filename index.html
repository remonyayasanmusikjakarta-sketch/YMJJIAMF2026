Welcome

HOME | Legacy in Motion

After navigating through global uncertainty and a long pause, Jakarta International Summer Music Festival returns in 2026 with renewed spirit, vision, and direction.

HOME | Legacy in Motion is not merely the return of a music camp — it is the continuation of a living and growing educational legacy. For years, JISMF has been a space for character formation, discipline, and international artistic standards for young Indonesian musicians.

The 2026 edition holds special significance. JISMF alumni — who have now completed studies at various world-renowned music institutions — return as faculty members. The place that was once their learning space now becomes a space of dedication and mentoring for the next generation.

Carrying the philosophy of Roots & Wings, JISMF 2026 celebrates strong educational roots while acknowledging the wings that carry students to international stages — and now, returning to build Indonesia's musical ecosystem.

For one full week, participants will undergo an intensive artistic experience: living together in a creative community, attending two to three major instrument lessons and masterclasses, and performing in concerts every evening alongside fellow participants and faculty. The festival culminates with the Indonesia Open Competition (IOC) — an open competition upholding transparency and international standards — and closes with a Gala Concert celebrating artistic achievement and excellence.

About

Programme

Designed for Competition Readiness

JISMF 2026 is designed as an intensive training programme to help young musicians prepare for music competitions at an international level.

Unlike conventional festivals, JISMF presents a focused learning ecosystem where participants not only receive technical guidance, but also develop artistic strategies, mental readiness, and a deep understanding of global competition standards.

The programme features JISMF alumni who are now active on international stages as faculty, offering real and relevant perspectives drawn from their direct experience.

The Core of JISMF

Performance

Internal Concert Series

Participants will perform in concerts every evening alongside fellow participants and faculty — designed as structured performance simulations before competitions.

Each performance serves as a vital step in developing stage readiness, confidence, and artistic maturity.

Methodology

Continuous Learning Cycle

Perform
Evaluate
Refine
Perform Again

After each internal concert, participants join in-depth evaluation sessions with faculty, discussing specific aspects of artistry, technique, and stage presentation. Through this cycle, participants gain multiple performance opportunities in a supportive environment, direct and applicable evaluation, space to implement real improvements, and measurable progress from one performance to the next.

Faculty Who Walked This Path

One of the greatest strengths of JISMF 2026 lies in its faculty — alumni of the festival who have now graduated from international music institutions and are active as performers and educators.

2
