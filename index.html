About The Festival

Our Legacy

After years of global uncertainty and pause, the Jakarta International Summer Music Festival returns in 2026 with renewed clarity of purpose and artistic conviction. HOME | Legacy in Motion marks not merely the revival of a music camp, but the continuation of a living legacy.

Our Philosophy

Under the philosophical framework of Roots & Wings, JISMF 2026 celebrates the educational lineage of YMJ — nurturing foundations strong enough to send students to the world, and welcoming them home to shape the next generation.

Dr. Kuei Pin Yeo

Artistic Director, JISMF 2026

"It is with deep gratitude and conviction that I welcome the return of the Jakarta International Summer Music Festival in 2026. This year is especially significant. JISMF alumni, now graduates of leading international music institutions, return as faculty members. This is the true spirit of HOME | Legacy in Motion — where strong roots give rise to wings, and those wings return to strengthen the roots of the next generation."

JISMF Alumni

A defining strength of JISMF 2026 lies in its faculty — distinguished alumni of the festival who have since graduated from leading international institutions and are now active artists and educators. Their return represents more than professional achievement; it represents continuity of vision.

Festival Program

Day 1

Opening Ceremony & Welcome Concert

The festival begins with an opening ceremony featuring performances by faculty members and a welcome reception for all participants.

Day 2-5

Intensive Lessons & Masterclasses

Participants will receive intensive lessons and masterclasses two to three times during the festival, engage in daily artistic dialogue with faculty.

Day 2-6

Evening Concerts

Nightly concerts featuring participants, faculty, and guest artists. Music becomes not only study, but lived experience.

Day 3-5

Workshops & Seminars

Specialized workshops on topics ranging from performance practice to career development in the modern music industry.

Day 6-7

Indonesia Open Competition

The festival culminates in the Indonesia Open Competition (IOC), a platform designed to uphold transparency, rigor, and international standards.

Day 7

Gala Concert & Closing Ceremony

Winners of the competition will be presented in a distinguished Gala Concert — a celebration of excellence and emerging artistry.

Workshops & Seminars

Classical Roots, Commercial Future

"Beyond the Conservatory: Entering the Modern Music Industry"

Explore the transition from classical training to diverse career paths in the contemporary music landscape.

European Music Schools

"The European Conservatoire System: Strengths, Challenges & Hidden Realities"

Indonesia Open Competition

About the Competition

The festival culminates in the Indonesia Open Competition (IOC), a platform designed to uphold transparency, rigor, and international standards. The competition provides an opportunity for participants to showcase their skills and receive valuable feedback from our distinguished international faculty.

Winners will be presented in a distinguished Gala Concert — a celebration of excellence and emerging artistry.

Competition Categories

  • Piano
  • Violin
  • Cello
  • Flute
  • Clarinet
  • Composition

Each category will have different age groups and repertoire requirements. Detailed competition guidelines will be provided upon registration.
